About Not For Sale

Origin Story

A movement born from a story too urgent to ignore.

In 2006, our co-founder David Batstone discovered that his favourite restaurant in San Francisco was trafficking young girls. He refused to look away, and together with Mark Wexler, launched Not For Sale. What began as a response to one tragedy has grown into a global movement built on a single belief: no one is for sale.

Our Mission

Stop exploitation at its source.

We combat the upstream causes of human trafficking, poverty, lack of opportunity, and environmental destruction, before they put people at risk. Instead of only responding after harm, we build social enterprises and invest in communities so people have stable livelihoods, education, and safe environments.

And we never stop supporting survivors, with care, training, and pathways back to dignity and independence.

Our Vision

A world where no one is for sale.

A future where every person is free, every community is resilient, and every ecosystem is protected, so that exploitation has no root left to grow from.

We measure success not by how many we rescue, but by how many never have to be.

Our Approach

Three connected fronts in one fight.

Human Trafficking

We prevent trafficking before it starts by tackling the vulnerabilities that make people targets, poverty, displacement, lack of education. Through long-term programs we provide protection, education, and opportunity so people can choose freedom over fear.

Social Enterprise

Poverty and lack of opportunity fuel exploitation. So we build mission-driven businesses that create fair jobs, sustainable income, and resilience, engines of dignity, not just lifelines.

Ecocide

Environmental destruction forces people into the desperate situations traffickers exploit. We protect ecosystems and empower communities to safeguard their land, forests, and rivers, breaking cycles of displacement.

Where We Work

We build long-term, sustainable projects across the world that address root causes and create lasting change.
